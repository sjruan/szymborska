S as Szymborska
A as Atropos

S: Madam Atropos?
A: That’s correct.
‍
S: Of Necesssity’s three daughters, you fare the worst in world opinion.
A: A gross exaggeration, my dear poet. Clotho spins the threat of life, but the thread is delicate and easily cut. Lachesis determines its length with her rod.They are no angels.
‍
S: Still you, Madame, hold the scissors.
A: And since I do, I put them to good use.

S: I see that even as we speak ...
A: I’m a Type A, that’s my nature.
‍
S: You don’t get bored or tired,maybe drowsy working nights?
Really, not the slightest?With no holidays, vacations,weekends,
no quick breaks for cigarettes?
A: We’d fall behind, I don’t like that.

S: Such breathtaking industry.But you’re not given commendations, orders, trophies, cups, awards? Maybe just a framed diploma?
A: Like at the hairdresser’s? No, thank you.

S: Who, if anyone, assists you?
A: A tidy little paradox—you mortals.Assorted dictators, untold fanatics.Not that they need me to nudge them.They’re eager to get down to work.
‍
S: Wars must surely make you happy what with all the assistance you receive.
A: Happy? I don’t know the feeling.I’m not the one who declares them, I’m not the one who steers their course. I will admit, though, that I’m grateful, they do help to keep me au courant.

S: You’re not sorry for the threads cut short?
A: A little shorter, a lot shorter—Only you perceive the difference.

S: And if someone stronger wanted to relieve you,tried to make you take retirement?
A: I don’t follow. Express yourself more clearly.

S: I’ll try once more: do you have a Higher-Up?
A: ...Next question please.

S: That’s all I’ve got.
A: Well goodbye then. Or to put it more precisely ...

S: I know, I know. Au revoir.
