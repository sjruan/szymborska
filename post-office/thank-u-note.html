Dear Sir or Madam:

I owe a great deal to you. But knowing that you are dearer to someone else makes me such a relief. I’m glad that I am not the wolf to your sheep.

When I with you, peace and freedom unto me, this is something that love cannot give or take away.  I don’t need to wait you from window to door, my patient almost like a sundial. When I with you, I understand what love doesn’t understand, I forgive what love would never forgive.

It’s just merely a few days or weeks to me from meeting to letter, not an eternal torture. The trip with you always turn out well, concerts heard, cathedrals visited, landscapes in sharp focus. When we are separated by seven mountains and rivers, they are just mountains and rivers well known from any map, which doesn’t upset me at all. I live in three dimensions, in a non-lyrical and non-rhetorical space with a shifting and real horizon, because of you.

You don’t know how much you carry in your empty hands. You know when it comes to love, it would just say “ I owe them nothing ”.


Sincerely & Best Regards.
Szymborska, 1976

A Large Number (1976)

I owe so much
to those I don’t love.
‍
The relief as I agree
that someone else needs them more.

The happiness that I’m not
the wolf to their sheep.
The peace I feel with them,
the freedom –
love can neither given
or take that.

I don’t wait for them,
as in window-to-door-and-back.
Almost as patient
as a sundial,
I understand
what love can’t,
and forgive
as love never would.
‍
From a rendezvous to a letter
is just a few days or weeks,
not an eternity.
‍
Trips with them always go smoothly,
concerts are heard,
cathedrals visited,
scenery is seen.

And when seven hills and rivers
come between us,
the hills and rivers
can be found on any map.
‍
They deserve the credit
if I live in three dimensions,
in nonlyrical and nonrhetorical space
with a genuine, shifting horizon.

They themselves don’t realize
how much they hold in their empty hands.

“I don’t owe them a thing,”
would be love’s answer
to this open question.

–Translated by Stanisław Barańczak and Clare Cavanagh
